# Concepts

## Legibility

Legibility is the degree to which a capability can explain itself to a person or agent without requiring tribal knowledge. A legible capability makes its business intent, constraints, dependencies, and exceptions visible in the artifacts the system already exposes.

## Capability

A capability is a named business behavior that matters to the organization. It is the unit you can reason about, contract, validate, and place in a dependency graph.

## Contract

A contract is the machine-readable declaration of what a capability does, what it needs, what it promises, and what it does not do. The contract is the authoritative navigation surface for that capability.

## Capability Graph

The capability graph is the directed map of how contracts depend on one another. It lets a practitioner or agent see upstream and downstream relationships before changing behavior.
